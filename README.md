# Logistic-Shipment (From Dispatch to Delivery: Logistics Performance & Shipment Intelligence)
### This Repository has Logistic Shipment projects.

"From Origin to Destination — Every Shipment Tells a Story Worth Analyzing"


# Project Overview

Logistic Shipment Dashboard is an end-to-end supply chain analytics project built in Microsoft Excel and Power Bi, analyzing global shipment operations for a logistics company across 2023 and 2024. The dataset covers 5,000 total shipments across 10 countries and 4 product categories, tracking revenue, cost, delivery performance, shipment status, and individual salesperson contributions.
The project features a fully interactive Dashboard with category-level and country-level filter slicers — enabling dynamic exploration of shipment trends by product type, geography, and time period. It is designed to give management a 360° operational and financial view of global logistics performance.

This project provides a comprehensive analysis of logistics and shipment operations, focusing on revenue, cost, shipment status, delivery performance, and geographic distribution.
The interactive dashboard enables stakeholders to monitor end-to-end shipment lifecycle, identify operational bottlenecks, and optimize logistics performance.

Users can explore data dynamically across Year, Product Category, and Geography, allowing for deeper insights into shipment trends and operational efficiency.



## Business Objectives
- Track overall revenue, cost, and shipment volume
- Monitor shipment status (Active, Completed, Returned)
- Analyze monthly revenue trends
- Evaluate geographic performance of shipments
- Measure salesperson performance and efficiency
- Identify opportunities to improve delivery success rate and cost efficiency


## Key KPIs
- Total Revenue: $2.74M
- Total Cost: $1.21M
- Total Shipments: 5K
- Completed Shipments: 3K (62%)
- Active Shipments: 2K (33%)
- Returned Shipments: 248 (5%)

# Detailed Insights

1) Shipment Status Analysis

  - Majority of shipments are completed (62%), indicating stable operations.
  - 33% shipments are still active, suggesting pipeline volume or possible delays.
  - 5% returns highlight manageable but important reverse logistics costs.


2) Monthly Revenue Trend

  - Revenue remains relatively stable throughout the year, ranging between ~$186K–$244K.
  - Peak revenue observed around May and September (~$244K).
  - Slight dip in February (~$186K).


3) Category Contribution

  - Electronics is the top revenue-generating category at 40% ($1.10M), followed by Audio at 29% ($801K), Computing at 21% ($567K), and Office Equipment at 10% ($272K). Despite being the revenue leader, Electronics should be examined for margin quality — high revenue doesn't always mean high profitability, especially in competitive electronics categories.
  - Followed by Audio (29%) and Computing (21%).
  - Office Equipment (10%) has the lowest share.


4) Geographic Performance

  - India, Australia, and Germany are top-performing regions.
  - Countries like UK and China contribute relatively less.

    
5️) Salesperson Performance

  - Salespersons show balanced shipment handling and completion rates.
  - Some individuals have higher completed shipments with fewer returns, indicating efficiency.


6️) Returns Analysis

  - Return rate is relatively low (5%), which is positive.
  - However, even small return percentages can significantly impact cost and customer satisfaction.


7) India Leads Global Shipment Volume

  - India tops the shipment geography with 541 shipments, closely followed by Australia (529), Germany (523), and Japan (516). This near-even global distribution across all 10 markets is a strength — the business is not over-reliant on a single country. China ranks lowest at 464 shipments, which may indicate untapped market potential.


8) Revenue Is Relatively Stable Month-Over-Month

  - CMonthly revenue ranges from a low of $185.8K (February) to a high of $244.3K (March and October), with no dramatic peaks or valleys. This relatively flat monthly revenue curve suggests consistent demand throughout the year, though February's dip warrants attention — it may be driven by fewer shipping days or seasonal demand softness.


9) Bonnie Lucero Leads in Total Shipments

  - Among all 10 sales representatives, Bonnie Lucero leads with 286 total shipments (88 active, 179 completed, 19 returned), followed by Kayla Banks (270) and Jessica White (269). At the other end, Sandra Henderson handles the fewest shipments (254) — though her completion-to-active ratio should also be examined for quality.


10) "Stacey Brown" Has the Highest Active Shipment Count and "Jessica White" Has the Lowest Return Count

  - Stacey Brown has 101 active shipments — the highest on the team — while having only 155 completed and 8 returned. This high active count could indicate either a growing pipeline or potential delays in closing shipments, and warrants follow-up to ensure those shipments convert to completions rather than returns.
  - Jessica White has only 6 returned shipments out of 269 total — the lowest return rate on the team (2.2%). This is an indicator of high-quality order handling, accurate product matching, and reliable delivery execution. Her practices could serve as a best-practice model for the broader team.


11) Healthy Gross Margin of ~56%

  - With revenue of $2.74M against a cost of $1.21M, the business operates at a gross margin of approximately 56%. This is a strong margin for a logistics operation — though it should be tracked at the category and country level to ensure that high-volume but low-margin segments are not diluting overall profitability.




## Business Recommendations

1. Optimize Active Shipments
  - Monitor shipments in transit to reduce delays.
  - Implement real-time tracking and alerts for better visibility.


2. Reduce Return Rate
  - Analyze root causes of returned shipments.
  - Improve packaging, delivery accuracy, and customer communication.


3. Strengthen High-Performing Categories
  - Focus on Electronics and Audio for revenue growth.
  - Expand product offerings in these categories.


4. Geographic Expansion Strategy
  - Invest more in top-performing regions (India, Australia, Germany).
  - Improve logistics and partnerships in low-performing regions.

    
5. Improve Operational Efficiency
  - Benchmark high-performing salespersons and replicate their strategies.
  - Provide training for underperforming teams.


6. Revenue Optimization
  - Plan promotions during low-revenue months (e.g., February).
  - Leverage historical trends for forecasting and inventory planning.


7. Investigate and Reduce the 5% Return Rate
  - With 248 returned shipments, management should conduct a root cause analysis on all returns — categorizing them by reason (damaged goods, wrong item, customer refusal, address errors, etc.). Reducing the return rate from 5% to below 3% could recover significant revenue and reduce reverse logistics overhead.


8. Convert Active Shipments Before They Become Overdue
  - With 1,639 active shipments (33% of total), management should implement automated aging alerts for shipments that have been in "Active" status beyond a defined SLA threshold. Each aging active shipment is a risk of either becoming a return or a customer complaint.


9. Investigate February Revenue Dip
  - February is the lowest-revenue month at $185.8K — significantly below the annual average of ~$228K per month. Management should determine whether this is due to calendar effects (fewer shipping days), seasonal demand patterns, or operational slowdowns, and consider targeted promotional campaigns or pre-booking incentives to smooth February performance.


10. Balance Stacey Brown's Active Shipment Pipeline
  - Stacey Brown's 101 active shipments are significantly above the team average. Management should review the age and status of these shipments to ensure they are progressing toward completion — and provide support if there are systemic delays or customer communication issues holding them back.


11. Replicate Jessica White's Low Return Rate Practices
  - Jessica White's 2.2% return rate is the best on the team. Management should interview her on her pre-shipment verification process, customer communication practices, and order accuracy checks — then document and distribute these as standard operating procedures for the wider sales team.
